FAQ:

What's the difference between the Sprint and the Retainer?

The Sprint is a one-time, fixed-scope project, which is from an idea to shippable product in four to six weeks. The Retainer is an ongoing monthly partnership for products that keep growing. Most people start with a Sprint and move to a Retainer once there's something worth maintaining.

Do I get the design files when we're done?

Yes. Full handoff of source files and assets at the end of a Sprint, or at any point during a Retainer.

Can I cancel or pause the Retainer?

Yes. It is month-to-month, no long-term contract. Cancel or pause anytime with one month's notice.

How do we communicate during a project?

Async by default, either Slack or email. Higher retainer tiers include a weekly call and faster turnaround; details are scoped to what your product actually needs, not a fixed script.

Do you handle branding and logo design too?

I am generally focused on product design, UX, UI, and the systems around them. If a project needs a logo or brand identity as part of scoping a Sprint, that's a conversation to have upfront, not an assumption either way.
