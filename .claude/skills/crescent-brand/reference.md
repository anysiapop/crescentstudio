# Crescent Studio — Full Reference

Opened only when a task needs detail beyond SKILL.md's core rules.

## Origin & belief system

"A crescent is a fraction of the moon. Crescent Studio is a fraction of a design team — the fraction you actually need, showing on purpose."

Design used to mean bigger teams — more hands, more meetings, more people between the founder and the work. Crescent Studio started from a different premise: a founder doesn't need a department, they need one senior designer who actually knows their product and is still there next month. Senior taste. Fixed scope. Monthly return.

Beliefs:
- Great design was never about headcount — it was always about one person paying close enough attention.
- A retainer is a relationship, not a subscription.
- Scope is a form of respect — fixed scope, fixed price, no surprises, for both sides.
- Restraint is part of the craft — shipping three screens that work beats shipping five that don't.
- A crescent is a moon caught mid-change — not done, not nothing, in progress on purpose.
- The moon has no light of its own — we're not inventing what a founder wants, we're reflecting it back sharper.

## Positioning

Crescent Studio is a fractional product design partner for early-stage B2B founders who treat design as an investment, not an expense. We work on retainer — monthly, ongoing, embedded in the product — bringing the strategy, UX, and UI a founder needs without the cost, timeline, or risk of a hire.

On the Sprint: an eclipse isn't the sun disappearing, it's alignment — one body passing exactly in front of another for a short, precise window. The MVP Sprint is total, focused overlap for six weeks. Then the studio moves on, and the founder keeps the light.

## Brand personality

If Crescent Studio were a person: shows up the same time every month, reliable before impressive. Asks about the business before opening the design file. Says no to scope creep. Picks one strong direction over five options. Explains a decision in one sentence. Stays calm when a founder is moving fast.

## Six post formats

01 The Reframe — challenge a hiring/design assumption, one line of reframe, brand close.
02 The Observation — one clean insight, no setup, 1–2 sentences.
03 The Principle — one line pulled from values, stands alone.
04 The Work Caption — client context, the tension, what actually shipped.
05 The Process Note — a single decision or trade-off from inside the work.
06 The Question — one open question, no answer given.

## Platform guidance

LinkedIn (primary): open with tension, never "I" or "we." Three to five short paragraphs is fine. Close with a question only if it genuinely invites a reply.
Email: short, one idea per email, sign off with the closing statement.
Proposals & website: lead with scope and outcome, state price and timeline plainly, close with the brand promise.

## Case study / work posts with real images

A real product screenshot can't be art-directed to match the palette — so the discipline moves to the frame, not the content. Two approved treatments:
- The moon window: contain the screenshot inside the arched frame shape. Caption underneath in Work Caption format.
- Full-bleed with a solid ink caption strip at the bottom (never text floating over the image) — keeps captions legible regardless of the screenshot's own colors.

## Motion language

"We move like a phase, not a flash." No bounce, no spring, nothing that arrives faster than it should.
Emerge — slow ease-in, nothing appears instantly.
Hold — stillness is allowed; a held frame reads as confidence.
Return — transitions fade and echo rather than cut; the same shape reappears rather than a new one replacing it.

## Campaign style

Everyday brand touchpoints stay quiet on purpose so a launch or campaign push feels like something. Campaigns are allowed the full eclipse gradient, a bigger headline, one image doing all the work. Rule: a campaign look is retired when the campaign ends — it doesn't graduate into the standing system just because it worked once.
