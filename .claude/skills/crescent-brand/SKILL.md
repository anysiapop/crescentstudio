# Crescent Studio — Brand Skill

Fractional product design studio for early-stage B2B founders. Core line: "Your designer. Monthly." Brand essence: "Fractional, not diminished."

For anything beyond the rules below — full statement bank, headline system, social post formats, platform guidance, motion language, campaign style, the manifesto — read `reference.md` in this same skill folder.

## Color

Neutrals: Eclipse Black `#0B0B0B` · Moon White `#F5F4EF` · Lunar Grey `#A7A7A7` · Moon Background `#F1E9DE`

The one standing accent: Sunset Terracotta `#C1623B`. Nothing else is a general-purpose accent color.

Terracotta ramp (use instead of picking a new orange):
`#F1DAD0 → #E2B4A1 → #D2896B → #C1623B (base) → #9C4F30 → #753C24 → #4E2818`

Lavender `#E2C2FF` / indigo is the baby sibling of the standing accent. Appears every once in a while, only as the one ingredient.

The full spectrum gradient:

Full Spectrum (Lavender → Sunsets → Black):
linear-gradient(160deg, #E2C2FF 0%, #D2896B 19%, #C1623B 49%, #0B0B0B 100%)

Two terracotta-only gradients are safe for everyday use — ramp stops only, never a new hue:
linear-gradient(180deg, #D2896B 0%, #6B3A2E 100%) — Sunset Gradient
linear-gradient(180deg, #E2B4A1 0%, #9C4F30 100%) — Golden Sunset

## Typography

Primary — PP Neue Montreal: everything functional (body copy, labels, UI, captions).
Secondary — Times New Roman: everything with voice (headlines, pull quotes, the manifesto).
No third typeface. Two fonts, each with a job the other doesn't do.

## Voice — six qualities

Clear, Human, Confident, Precise, Steady, Honest. In four words: "Clear. Human. Confident. Steady."

Five writing rules:
1. Short sentences carry more trust.
2. Never open with "I" or "we" — open with the founder's problem.
3. Cut the adverb, keep the verb.
4. The last line is what they act on.
5. One word can be a sentence when it's earned.

Vocabulary — use: ship, scope, senior, steady, embedded, considered, return, ready, taste, partner, clarity, retained.
Vocabulary — avoid: leverage, synergy, solutions, holistic, scalable, disruptive, ecosystem, robust, cutting-edge, seamless, passionate, utilize.

## Visual rules

1. Flat surfaces only — no gradients, textures, or glass, except the one locked eclipse asset.
2. One accent, used as a line — never a background, except the eclipse.
3. The crescent mark carries the meaning — reach for it before any new shape or icon.
4. Whitespace is a decision, not empty space to fill.
5. Hierarchy comes from type, not decoration — no boxes, icons, or color-coding to organize a page.

## Shapes

The crescent mark: white on black or black on cream, never colored or gradient-filled. Used alone, most of the time.
The echo contour: thin concentric arcs from the crescent's curve, rare textural accent only.
The moon window: an arched, domed frame, reserved for photography, pull quotes, section breaks. Rarest shape.
Cut from the system: the organic blob — too generic, carries no meaning the crescent doesn't already carry.

## Photography

Natural, directional light, not studio-flat. Real founders and real workspaces, not stock-photo energy. Close, considered crops. Black or cream backgrounds where possible so photography sits inside the palette.

## Social posting discipline

Roughly 1 tile in 12 is allowed to be the signature eclipse moment. Everything else is flat black or cream, the mark, and a line of copy. No two tiles doing the same rich/glowing treatment in the same rotation — a treatment either earns a permanent place or gets retired.
